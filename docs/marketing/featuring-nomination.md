## Why Now

Turn Timer is moving from a simple visual timer into a reusable visual time
management tool. The launch update centers the product around templates,
rounds, saved routines, Pro reuse, iCloud sync, and Apple-native surfaces where
available in the submitted build.

## Editorial Angle

Many timers are built for one person staring at a number. Turn Timer makes the
sequence visible: who is up, what comes next, how much time remains, and which
routine can be reused tomorrow.

The launch story should start with game night fairness, then expand into
classrooms, cooking, meetings, focus routines, and household tasks.

## Apple Technology

## Pitch Draft

```text
Turn Timer makes time visible for real-world sequences: game turns, recipe steps, classroom stations, meeting speakers, plant watering zones, and routines. The launch update turns a simple visual timer into an Apple-native reusable countdown tool with starter templates, saved Turn Timer files, private iCloud sync, and Pro reuse features.

The strongest wedge is game night fairness: everyone can see whose turn is active, what comes next, and how much time remains. From there, the same colorful round model helps teachers rotate stations, cooks reuse recipe timers, teams keep agendas honest, and families run everyday routines without rebuilding timers from scratch.

Turn Timer is built with SwiftUI, StoreKit 2, CloudKit, and other Apple frameworks, and it avoids third-party tracking, ads, and analytics SDKs in the current implementation.
```
